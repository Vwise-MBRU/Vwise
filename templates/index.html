  1. Purpose of the Research Study and Overview of Participation

    2. You are invited to participate in a research study in which you will interact with a conversational agent, or chatbot. The overall aim of this study is to understand how a chatbot might be used as a health communication tool to address people’s hesitancy to vaccinate.

    Prior to interacting with the chatbot you will be asked to answer 10 questions to better understand your opinion about vaccination, as well some demographic information (age, gender, nationality and vaccination status). No personal information (e.g., name, ID, etc) will be collected.

    The chatbot is designed to answer questions about COVID-19 and vaccinations. You will be able to ask questions and interact with the bot for as long as you would like. After you have finished talking to the chatbot, you will be asked to fill out a short survey about your experience with the bot and repeat the original survey of 10 questions.

    The entire experience should take about 10-15 minutes (or longer should you want to continue interacting with the bot).

    We are committed to protecting your privacy. Any information you share with the chatbot will be accessible ONLY to the research team and will only be used for research purposes. Any information shared with the chatbot that might identify you in some way will be removed from the dataset and will not be included in any reports.

  2. Any Risks as a Result of Participating in the Study

    3. You might feel worried or anxious when discussing/asking questions regarding the Covid-19 pandemic or vaccines. Please know that your participation is voluntary and that you can stop and leave at any given moment without any consequence or penalty.

  3. Any Benefits as a Result of Participating in the Study

    4. The chatbot prototype that you will be conversing with is developed using information about COVID-19 and vaccines that is gathered from trusted sources such as the WHO. Based on the questions you ask it you will be given information that is tailored to your need. By participating in this study, you can help us contribute to the body of knowledge about vaccine hesitancy, and to contribute to the development of an innovative tool that can be utilized to improve health communication.

If you agree to take part in this research study, please, be ensured that the obtained information will be kept confidential. Unless required by law, only the study investigator or designee, the MBRU-Institutional Review Board Committee (MBRU-IRB), and/or inspectors from governmental agencies will have direct access to your information.
